Europe Pass

Interrail

No introduction needed — and yet, so many misconceptions! If you thought the Interrail pass was only for young people, think again. There are four age categories, and if you plan more than one return trip, it'll always be worth it.

Pro tip: try to buy your pass during sale periods (usually twice a year: winter and summer). Once purchased, you have a year to activate it.

If you're 27: a youth pass must be activated before your 28th birthday, even if you bought it less than a year ago.

Reservations: some trains (especially in France and Germany in summer) require a paid seat reservation. Book via raileurope.com. The Interrail Rail Planner app shows whether one is needed.

The Rail Planner app doesn't always highlight night trains — tick "night trains only" in the filters.

If you pass through Switzerland, minimise connections — Swiss rail is famously punctual.

Night on the Rails

Night Trains

To make the most of your travel time, the best option is to sleep on the rails! A sleeper berth is admittedly more expensive, but when you subtract the hotel night you've saved, it's genuinely good value. And with an Interrail pass, just ~€20 in booking fees on top.

Night train + Interrail tip: if you've been travelling all day and board a night train that same evening, you don't need to activate an extra travel day. An excellent deal.

For all night train news and the connections map: back-on-track.eu
